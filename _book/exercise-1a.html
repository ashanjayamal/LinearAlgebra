Linear Algebra

Chapter 2 Exercise 1A

  1. Show that \(\alpha + \beta = \beta + \alpha\) for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}\).

  1. Show that \((\alpha + \beta) + \lambda = \alpha + (\beta + \lambda)\) for all \(\alpha, \beta, \lambda \in \mathbb{C}\).

  1. Show that \((\alpha \beta)\lambda = \alpha(\beta \lambda)\) for all \(\alpha, \beta, \lambda \in \mathbb{C}\).

  2. Show that \(\lambda(\alpha + \beta) = \lambda\alpha + \lambda\beta\) for all \(\lambda, \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}\).

  3. Show that for every \(\alpha \in \mathbb{C}\), there exists a unique \(\beta \in \mathbb{C}\) such that \(\alpha + \beta = 0\).

  4. Show that for every \(\alpha \in \mathbb{C}\) with \(\alpha \neq 0\), there exists a unique \(\beta \in \mathbb{C}\) such that \(\alpha\beta = 1\).

  5. Show that \(\frac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}\) is a cube root of 1 (meaning that its cube equals 1).

  6. Find two distinct square roots of \(i\).

  7. Find \(x \in \mathbb{R}^4\) such that \((4, -3, 1, 7) + 2x = (5, 9, -6, 8)\).

  8. Explain why there does not exist \(\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\) such that \(\lambda(2 - 3i, 5 + 4i, -6 + 7i) = (12 - 5i, 7 + 22i, -32 - 9i)\).
